Research

Applied econometrics across ageing, labour, education and development

Eight connected research themes, united by a common approach: identify causal effects in longitudinal survey data, and translate the results for academic, government, consulting and policy audiences.

Methodological toolkit

Empirical methods used across this research

Method choice follows the identification problem, not the other way around. Across the themes below, the recurring tools are:

IV-2SLS Fixed Effects IV (FE-IV) Fuzzy Regression Discontinuity Staggered Difference-in-Differences Event-Study Designs Panel Fixed Effects Longitudinal Survey Analysis

Themes

Research areas

Applied economics

Economic theory turned into testable, policy-relevant questions — household decision-making, market-level analysis — in both academic and commercial settings.

Methods: panel fixed effects, instrumental variables, applied causal inference on survey and administrative data.

Labour economics and retirement

Statutory retirement ages provide the variation: how retirement timing and occupational context shape functional independence, health behaviour, and wellbeing in later working life, and why the effect differs by occupation.

Methods: IV-2SLS around statutory retirement ages, fuzzy regression discontinuity, occupation-stratified estimation.

Economics of ageing

Determinants of healthy ageing — grip strength and functional health, informal caregiving, locus of control, hope, emotional closeness, life expectancy — in cross-national European and Australian ageing cohorts.

Methods: longitudinal panel models, fixed-effects IV, event-study designs around policy and life-course transitions.

Education and human capital

Education as a determinant of later-life outcomes — instrumental activities of daily living, caregiving burden, marriage costs — and as a channel for early childhood development in Jordanian household surveys.

Methods: IV-2SLS, fixed-effects IV, human-capital production-function estimation.

Development economics

Household-level questions in Jordan — intra-household bargaining after parenthood, household size and domestic violence, household conditions and early childhood development — from demographic and health survey data.

Methods: panel and repeated cross-section estimation, instrumental variables, household bargaining models.

Policy evaluation

Retirement-policy governance and its effect on functional health; preventive aged-care and obesity policy for older Australians; closing-the-gap implications for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander adults.

Methods: staggered difference-in-differences, event-study designs, policy-variation identification across countries and cohorts.

Applied econometrics and causal inference

The methodological core of the work: identification strategies built to hold up to scrutiny, from instrument validity to parallel-trends testing, implemented in Python, R, and Stata.

Methods: IV-2SLS, FE-IV, fuzzy RDD, staggered DID, event-study designs.

Longitudinal survey data and empirical research

Working knowledge of major longitudinal and cross-national survey infrastructures, and the measurement, attrition, and harmonisation issues that come with them.

Datasets: SHARE (Europe), HILDA and ALSWH (Australia), JLMPS and JPFHS/DHS (Jordan).
